Patterns of the Top 10%

Five things every winner does — and losers don't.

i

A point-of-view headline, not a description.

Losers say "Accent Wall Services in Houston." Winners say "If you're here, you're not looking for basic," or "Texas leader in high-end wall decor." They make a claim the buyer can react to. CK's current hero makes none.

ii

A named, visible process.

Oakmond: Consult → Create → Install. Lively: a vocabulary of proportion, symmetry, lighting, texture, balance. The method is shown. It reframes "contractor" as "artist with a system." CK has a process but doesn't expose it.

iii

Material and technique vocabulary as expertise signal.

Lively lists seven material categories. Mulder owns Venetian plaster and custom resin. Buyers spending $5,000+ want to feel they're hiring an expert, not a handyman — the site has to speak their language.

iv

Press and third-party validation on-site.

Mulder's Scout Guide / Canvas Rebel / Bold Journey mentions are the single biggest move up the premium ladder. Any earned mention belongs above the fold, not buried. CK's page has a blank slot waiting for this.

v

One primary CTA, repeated.

Every top site leans on one action — "Book a consultation" or "Discuss your vision." Courses, phone calls, email exist, but only one is the hero CTA. CK currently splits between consultation and courses with equal weight; that's a conversion leak.

Recommended Design Direction

Where we'll take CK, and why.

Every recommendation below is backed by a specific gap or opportunity surfaced in this analysis. Nothing here is a stylistic preference — it is a competitive move.

Palette

Warm editorial — ink, antique gold, ivory

Keep CK's existing logo palette (#0A0A0A · #C9A961 · #F6F1EA), add terracotta (#C45D3E) as a reactive accent. This widens the gap from competitors stuck on pure black-and-gold.

Typography

Instrument Serif (display) + DM Sans (body)

Editorial serif for the voice a category leader owns; humanist sans for calm body copy. Instantly differentiates from every competitor using default Wix/Squarespace sans stacks.

Motion Language

Cinematic scroll with GSAP + ScrollTrigger

Every section transitions with intent. Hero reserves space for a 3D/video scroll asset. Parallax on portfolio. Subtle micro-interactions on CTAs. Nobody in the category has this.

Information Architecture

Portfolio categorized by technique + room

Wainscoting · Geometric · Shiplap · Herringbone · Board & Batten · Ceiling · Glitter · Fine Paints. Doubles as SEO surface.

Hero Statement

"Walls that make the house."

Three headline options tested in the Build Brief. Every option is a claim, not a description — directly addressing Pattern I.

The CK Method

Three visible steps: Consult · Design · Install

Named, illustrated, scroll-animated. Closes the gap with Oakmond and claims the process narrative in Houston.

Single Hero CTA

"Book a Consultation"

Courses become a dedicated secondary track at /courses — not a competing button. Resolves Pattern V.

Trust Bar

Fine Paints of Europe · Press-ready · Founder-led

The FPE partnership moves from buried to branded; press band sits empty-but-ready so earned mentions drop in with zero engineering.
