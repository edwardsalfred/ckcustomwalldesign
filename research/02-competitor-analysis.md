## Patterns of the Top 10%

Five patterns separate the winners from the rest:

### 1. A Point-of-View Headline, Not a Description
Losers say "Accent Wall Services in Houston." Winners say "If you're here, you're not looking for basic" or "Texas leader in high-end wall decor." They make a claim the buyer can react to.

### 2. A Visible, Named Process
Oakmond: Consult → Create → Install. Lively: discusses proportion, symmetry, lighting. The method is named and shown — it transforms "contractor" into "artist + system."

### 3. Material / Technique Vocabulary as Expertise Signal
Lively lists 7 material categories. Mulder owns "venetian plaster" and "resin artwork." Buyers at this price point want to feel they're hiring an expert, not a handyman. The website has to speak the language.

### 4. Press & Third-Party Validation On-Site
Mulder's press mentions (Scout Guide, Canvas Rebel, Bold Journey) are the single biggest move up the premium ladder. Any earned mention goes at the top of the page, not buried.

### 5. One Primary CTA, Repeated
Every top site leans on **one** primary action — "Book a consultation" or "Discuss your vision." Courses, phone calls, email all exist, but only one is the hero CTA. CK's current site splits between consultation and courses at equal weight; that's a conversion leak.

## Design Direction Implications for CK

1. **Out-design everyone.** Oakmond is the highest-scoring competitor, and their site is still "premium contractor," not "editorial luxury." The ceiling of the category is low — a genuinely elegant site will dominate on design alone.
2. **Lead with Clinton & Kerry.** Nobody else in the category has the founder duo + pedigree story. That's the moat.
3. **Name the process.** "The CK Method" or similar — 3 steps, visualized, scroll-animated.
4. **Categorize the portfolio.** By technique (wainscoting, geometric, shiplap, herringbone…) AND by room. Oakmond still shows a dumb grid; this is table stakes at the top.
5. **Pull the Fine Paints of Europe partnership forward.** It's the single most defensible premium signal. Currently buried.
6. **Make the courses a separate track,** not competing with consultation. Consider /courses subsite language.
7. **Press slot ready for earned mentions.** Leave a "As seen in" band empty-but-ready, so it's a trivial update when coverage lands.
